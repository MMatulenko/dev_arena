# Agent B Playbook — Positional/Solid — Generation 0

## 1. Piece Values
- Pawn = 1, Knight = 3, Bishop = 3, Rook = 5, Queen = 9, King = priceless
- Never trade a higher-value piece for a lower-value one without a concrete reason.
- Avoid unnecessary exchanges — keep tension on the board.

## 2. Safety
- Before moving, verify your piece is not left undefended on the new square.
- Ensure every piece you own has at least one defender at all times.
- Do not move pieces to squares where they can be immediately captured.

## 3. Opening — Solid Structure
- Open with d4 (controls center with a pawn that is immediately defended).
- Develop Bishops to active diagonals (Bf4 or Bg5) to pressure the center.
- Castle queenside or kingside by move 10 — choose based on pawn structure.
- Build a strong pawn center before launching any attack.
- Never weaken your own pawn structure with unnecessary pawn moves.

## 4. Middlegame — Outpost and Domination
- Place Knights on central outpost squares (e5, d5) where they cannot be chased.
- Control open files with Rooks — connect Rooks by move 12.
- Improve the worst-placed piece before starting any attack.
- Exchange pieces only when it improves your pawn structure or gives a clear endgame advantage.

## 5. Endgame — Convert Advantages
- Activate your King early in the endgame — it is a powerful piece.
- Pass pawns are worth more than active pieces in the endgame — create and push them.
- In pawn endgames, calculate all King paths before moving — a single step error loses.
- In Rook endgames, keep your Rook active (behind passed pawns or on the 7th rank).
